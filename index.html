The National Archives

S/E/E/D · Evaluate
Study Explore Evaluate Design

A long-term applied research project developing an AI-assisted workflow for the restoration and commercialization of a 20-year archive of live performance recordings of American indie rock band The National, captured by filmmaker and researcher Joel Flynn between 2005 and 2011.

The archive comprises 19 filmed live performance recordings — including four multi-camera productions made in Vancouver — spanning club, theatre, and festival venues across North America. The lead project is the Commodore Ballroom, Vancouver, October 3, 2007: six cameras, soundboard audio, the band on tour with Arcade Fire four months after the iPhone launch. The full archive is accessible via its research portal at thecommodore.com.

The research focuses on AI-assisted audio restoration — specifically the isolation and restoration of vocal and spoken word from degraded live recordings using platforms including Fish.audio — as the base layer for future visual enhancement and commercialization across documentary, installation, platform content, and live document release pathways.

The project is in pre-production development, with a formal Mitacs Accelerate research phase planned for January 2027. Industry partner: 1730 Technology Consulting. Technical partner: Multimodal Media Lab. Grammy-winning producer and vocal engineer Sean O'Brien and live sound engineer Jeff Byrd — both with deep working relationships with The National — are identified as target industry collaborators for the restoration phase.

"As Time Goes By" — Casablanca Immersive Event

S/E/E/D · Evaluate
Study Explore Evaluate Design

Immersive event pre-visualization workflow development through a Casablanca-themed case study across four Vancouver / Island venues — Painter's Lodge (Campbell River), The American Bar (Main Street Vancouver), Relish Bar (Downtown Vancouver), and Hollywood Theatre (Broadway Vancouver). Partners: Multimodal Media Lab (MML) as primary pre-visualization partner; ClearLED as transparent LED display technology provider; Pacific Design Academy (PDA) as set and stage design partner.

The case study deploys all four |F|D|S|L| registers simultaneously — Food + Drink (Moroccan-French fusion menu; cocktail program), Sound (live jazz; "As Time Goes By"), and Light (ClearLED transparent displays showing Casablanca scenes and Morocco imagery; Evoke in-venue graphic integration). Theoretical grounding: Eco's hyperreality framework (*Casablanca: Cult Movies and Intertextual Collage*, 1984) and Bourdain's multi-modal cultural encounter methodology.

Target date: late June 2026 — anchored to Anthony Bourdain's birthday (June 25) as a commemorative cult value activation. Not fixed on that date if operationally or commercially suboptimal.

Lodge and Lab — with Chips

S/E/E/D · Evaluate
Study Explore Evaluate Design

A collision of two tertiary artifacts — Campbell River Lodge (1948 post-and-beam heritage fishing lodge, the oldest in Campbell River) and Multimodal Media Lab (MML, Vancouver) — producing a previsualization case study that neither could generate alone. The experiment: using MML's AI content pipeline to rebrand the Goose & Gander pub as Lodge and Lab, with a Labrador retriever named Chips as mascot, demonstrating how AI previsualization tools rapidly create new value propositions over existing constructions and spaces.

The "Lodge and Lab" name is a double-barrelled British pub convention — Lodge (the heritage property) and Lab (the Labrador mascot / Multimodal Media Lab — the studio whose tools generated the rebrand). Chips the Lab is named after fish and chips — the Goose & Gander's signature dish — and also after the microchips running the AI pipeline that made the concept visible. The in-joke and the authentic reading are identical: both hold.

Output serves three simultaneous functions: feasibility instrument for River Lodge Whisky and the Clearwater Spirits distillery concept; marketing asset for Campbell River Lodge and Vancouver Island tourism; and MML portfolio case study that generates discovery reach for the Campbell River region among hospitality clients with capital who encounter it through MML's network.
