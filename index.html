The Lab — an imagined world

Marx Wartofsky called them tertiary artifacts: imagined worlds that constitute a domain in which there is a free construction in the imagination of rules and operations different from those of ordinary practice — "off-line" relative to the actual world, but able to come back and restructure perception of it. The lab is one of those. It has had rooms. It will have more. The artifact persists across all of them.

John Seely Brown called it radical research — radical in the original Greek sense: follow the problem to its root, and be willing to bend your conceptual lenses accordingly. That is what happens here. When a lab project generates sufficient empirical grounding — when it crosses the VFD threshold — it migrates to projects.crooney.ca as a formal Mitacs application. Until then, it lives here.

Wartofsky (1979) · Models: Representation and the Scientific Understanding

"Such possible worlds may indeed reflect the limits of the perceptual praxis in a given 'actual' world — it is 'off-line' relative to a historically actual or dominant present mode of perceptual praxis. Once the visual picture can be 'lived in', perceptually, it can also come to color and change our perception of the actual world, as envisioning possibilities in it not presently recognized."

Marx W. Wartofsky · p.209

Route 66 — Travels in Intertextuality

Explore → Evaluate
Study Explore Evaluate Design

Route 66 is a 66-slide sequential portal into the full experimental research lineage — the number itself a content decision, the road metaphor inseparable from American rock and roll, both of which run through the research from 2006 to the present. Each slide functions as a container of content and/or a link to other objects: a designed sequence that is itself a playlist, itself a tertiary artifact.

The portal reaches backward to Travels in Intertextuality: The Autopoetic Identity of Remix Culture (Flynn 2006, SFU MASc thesis) — specifically to the Desolation Sound System (Artifact 5.12), an extended iPod audio piece constructed from recordings of a Campbell River fisherman, film samples, and songs by Jason Webley and the Pogues. Audio was the primary continuous layer; sequential images appeared at chapter-marker time codes, restructuring perception of the audio without displacing it. The first deployment of what the PhD would later theorize as the Sound and Light registers operating in coordination.

The portal reaches forward to Travels in Intertextuality '24 — 111 Soundtrap sessions seeded by the Bulwark podcast catalogue, the Seven Inning Stretch episodic series, and the Box Set 2026. Route 66 / Travels '24 is the explicit sequel to the Desolation Sound System: same structural logic, same intertextual travel metaphor, platform-shifted from iPod M4A chapter markers to Soundtrap and crooney.ca.

Presented at the Vancouver AI community on February 12, 2026. Personal research narrative — not on a Mitacs formalization path. In the sense that Anne-Laure Le Cunff means by becoming the scientist of your own life: this work has accomplished what it needed to accomplish at this phase without requiring a funding instrument to validate it.

The National Archives

S/E/E/D · Evaluate
Study Explore Evaluate Design

A long-term applied research project developing an AI-assisted workflow for the restoration and commercialization of a 20-year archive of live performance recordings of American indie rock band The National, captured by Joel Flynn between 2005 and 2011. The archive comprises 19 filmed live performances — including four multi-camera productions made in Vancouver — spanning club, theatre, and festival venues across North America.

The lead project is the Commodore Ballroom, Vancouver, October 3, 2007: six cameras, soundboard audio, the band on tour with Arcade Fire four months after the iPhone launch. The full archive is accessible via its research portal at thecommodore.com. The research focuses on AI-assisted audio restoration — specifically the isolation and restoration of vocal and spoken word from degraded live recordings using platforms including fish.audio — as the base layer for future visual enhancement and commercialization across documentary, installation, platform content, and live document release pathways.

Formal Mitacs Accelerate phase planned for January 2027. Industry partner: 1730 Technology Consulting Inc. Technical partner: Multimodal Media Lab. Grammy-winning producer and vocal engineer Sean O'Brien and live sound engineer Jeff Byrd — both with deep working relationships with The National — identified as target industry collaborators for the restoration phase.

"As Time Goes By" — Casablanca Immersive Event

S/E/E/D · Evaluate
Study Explore Evaluate Design

Immersive event pre-visualization workflow development through a Casablanca-themed case study across four Vancouver and Island venues — Painter's Lodge (Campbell River), The American Bar (Main Street Vancouver), Relish Bar (Downtown Vancouver), and Hollywood Theatre (Broadway Vancouver). Partners: Multimodal Media Lab as primary pre-visualization partner; ClearLED as transparent LED display technology provider; Pacific Design Academy as set and stage design partner.

The case study deploys all four |F|D|S|L| registers simultaneously — Food + Drink (Moroccan-French fusion menu; cocktail program), Sound (live jazz; "As Time Goes By"), and Light (ClearLED transparent displays showing Casablanca scenes and Morocco imagery; Evoke in-venue graphic integration). Theoretical grounding: Eco's hyperreality framework (Casablanca: Cult Movies and Intertextual Collage, 1984) and Bourdain's multi-modal cultural encounter methodology. Target activation: late June 2026, anchored to Anthony Bourdain's birthday (June 25) as a commemorative cult value activation.

MML Partnership · Hospitality Pre-Viz · Campbell River · Light Register

Lodge and Lab — with Chips

S/E/E/D · Evaluate
Study Explore Evaluate Design

A collision of two tertiary artifacts — Campbell River Lodge (1948 post-and-beam heritage fishing lodge, the oldest in Campbell River) and Multimodal Media Lab (Vancouver) — producing a previsualization case study that neither could generate alone. The experiment: using MML's AI content pipeline to rebrand the Goose & Gander pub as Lodge and Lab, with a Labrador retriever named Chips as mascot, demonstrating how AI previsualization tools rapidly create new value propositions over existing constructions and spaces.

The "Lodge and Lab" name is a double-barrelled British pub convention — Lodge (the heritage property) and Lab (the Labrador mascot / Multimodal Media Lab — the studio whose tools generated the rebrand). Chips is named after fish and chips — the Goose & Gander's signature dish — and after the microchips running the AI pipeline that made the concept visible. The in-joke and the authentic reading are identical: both hold. Output serves three simultaneous functions: feasibility instrument for River Lodge Whisky and the Clearwater Spirits distillery concept; marketing asset for Campbell River Lodge and Vancouver Island tourism; and MML portfolio case study generating discovery reach for the Campbell River region among hospitality clients.
